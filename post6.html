My most poetic side


"Emblematic and majestic being..."

From "Marvelous Dwellers" Series


I have presented marvelous dwellers from all over the world, but today I came home to set up the scene of this tale.

In our Andes mountains, in South America, there is a species that stars antique legends, takes part in an invaluable responsability in nature and owns many titles of being "the most..." in several things.



This criature seems to be able to stop the clock when it appears like by art of magic in the sky. His black and white tones show and hide among clouds and mountains, very playfulness, very misterious, very presumptuous... Viewers amaze upon the majestic presence of this marvelous dweller… Vultus Gryphus... or the Andean condor.

Andean Condor has several “awards” of being “the most”...

You probably already know the first fact I'm going to present: he is the biggest bird in the entire world: males use to reach almost 1.5 metres from the peak to the tail, 1 metre of height and incredibly 3 metres, give or take, from one wing to another. Males weigh between 10 and 15 k.

But that’s not all: Andean Condor is one of the birds that can live longer , around 75 years.

To continue the list, he is also the bird with the ability to fly higher than others! He can achieve 6.500 metres of altitude using vertical streams of warm air.

He is master of saving energy too: he can glide hundreds of metres with his wings unfolded and without moving a single muscle. He can hold this for more than 5 hours, and if he chooses the route well, he can cover more than 170km. It turns out that the effort of beating the wings is extremely harsh for the bird, so he spends at least 75% of that only in departure, remaining in a glide position the rest of the time and beating wings once in a while.

Andean condors fulfill an important role in the ecosystem.

They are scavengers, meaning that they tear apart skin and tissue of dead animals to feed with. This special and nasty task helps minor scavengers to do their part in the process, because Condors can open the belly directly, given access to inner organs faster. Otherwise, minor scavengers has "a long rode to ride" but, chill out, I will reserve the gross details for "my daskest side". So, if Condor is near the "end of the existance" of another animal, we can say that it woukd be a feast for all the scavengers in the field!

Even though this sounds abhorrent indeed, it allows the erasing of the signs of death of other species and the prevention of the spread of some kind of bacterias that could harm humans or cause diseases. Thanks Condors!!!
A single condor can eat 5 kilos of meat per day, but also they can fast for five weeks. Their ability to view at long distance is paramount for holding their feeding.

Maybe their remarkable skills and the opportunity to contemplate them very high in the sky, where no other species can, were the reasons to inspire several cultures to consider the andean condor as something else… This marvelous dweller is a symbol of wisdom, strength, loyalty and greatness.

A sacred neverending life

The Incas believed that andean condor was who delivered their prayers to the Gods, so they considered it as sacred. Also, they thought that Condor was immortal. The myth said that when the condor was old and was running out of strength, he posed on the summit of the highest mountain and left himself free falling, giving place to the symbolic death and rebirding in the nest...

Lord of life, sun and mountains

The Aymaras believed that Condor was the spirit of the mountains and from there he can dominate the sources needed for life. They defend the belief that Condor delivered the water from the Andes to make plants able to grow and that he was responsible for the sun rising to the sky every day, that he could take it and move it over the mountains with his unstoppable energy…. For them, Condor represented the source of life.

And there is more! A legend tells that the world had been destroyed by water and fire in a huge storm and that the Andean Condor had been the only one survivor, thanks to his mastery in flying skills that made it possible to avoid the catastrophe. Thus, his supposed longevity in the universe would explained not only the wrinkles and baldness of Andean Condor, but also why he is respected as a wise being in Earth: he saw the birth of a new world, the world in which we live now…

Andean condor is considered as a cultural and natural heritage of all South America and, at the same time, in Argentina at least, this species is in the vulnerable category. In Mendoza, Santa Cruz and Tierra del Fuego is declared a Natural Monument and protected by Provincial Law for the conservation of native fauna. There are natural reserves of Condor and captive breeding programs in many countries. Accordingly the source, their definitive release to the environment leads to satellite monitoring through NASA transmitters with updated delivery of position data and monitoring by experts.

I don’t know if nowadays somebody shares the whole and charming idea behind the Condor that our ancestors had, but what it’s true is that when a Condor appears over you in the mountain, you stop to enjoy it. You follow his flying with your head second by second. You stay there until he’s gone. You feel blessed.
Maybe we do hold in our hearts all the apreciation, the admiration and the feelings about the Condor that older populations conveyed generation by generation.

There are some more curiosities about Condor that are worthies to explore!

For instance, for my total surprise, they are mute. They have their syrinx (vocal organ in birds) damaged. I bet that you didn’t know that, right!? Me neither, clearly!

Another thing is that Andean condor use to defecate or urinate over himself in order to "refresh" the high temperature of his paws, mainly. This process is called "urohydrosis", and is the reason why his legs are often dyed white (due to the uric acid). This practice is common among Vultures.

Of course I wouln't like you to remember the Andean Condor as a nasty species that pee or poop on himself, so I want to close with something romantic to balance the story.
Andean condors are loyals to their couple. They are monogamous and make real the expression “until death breaks us apart”.. He may have an ugly face but the manners of a sweet and romantic gentleman can conquer anyone! Here we have "a romantic kiss (with bad breath, definitely)".
