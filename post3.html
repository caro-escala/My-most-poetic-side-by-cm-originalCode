"The Light That Matters"

From "Marvelous Dwellers" Series


“Lumos” - she said, and the entire forest lit up under the powerful spell of her magic wand.

Of course, I’m bringing Hermione Granger here, to help me to introduce the real stars of my new post. But, chill out, we won’t talk about the hairy girl that thinks she knows everything in the world and that she is right all the time… This story lacks dementors and all the names can be said.

We will be delighted by authentic magicians of our real world.

To appreciate them properly, though, is paramount that you join me in a journey of wishful-thinkings…

"...envision yourself in a bountiful forest, alone in the night…
While the sounds of millions of bugs there don’t disturb the quietness of the place, they do hamper your inner peace.
The deep darkness starts to make you feel uneasy.
We are too used to relying on our sight, and now you can’t trust in what you see...
The time happens, and your unsettlement evolves to real fear.
Your heartbeat rises. You start to sweat.
Who is there?
Or even worse, what is there????

All of a sudden, thousands of tiny points of light turn on and the dreaded forest become something more alike to
an endless Christmas tree…"

It turns out that insects Lampyridae live in this forest!!!

Outside the scientific field, they are known as fireflies, glowworms, or lightning bugs, but they are actually winged beetles.

As if they were well-trained pupils of Prof. Filius Flitwick in the subject "Enchanments", they are able to enlighten themselves, but the production of colour is chemically based, not magic, of course. Specifically, the glowing colour appears in their lower abdomen, where they have the enzyme luciferase. In the presence of oxygen it oxidizes the luciferin pigment, resulting in oxyluciferin and light, and giving life to a phosphorescent organ. This "spell" is called bioluminescence and requires the enzymatic removal of two phosphate groups from ATP (adenosine triphosphate) to form AMP (adenosine monophosphate), which is almost the same way our cells get energy.

The wavelength emitted by the fireflies ranges between 510 and 670 nanometers, according to the species, meaning that they can show a pale yellow, reddish or light green contrasting the darkness of the night.
But the most astonishing thing is that the energy of this chemical reaction is transformed almost 100% in luminic energy, guaranteeing them the title of "master electricians".

Upon the absence of makeup, fireflies take advantage of bioluminescence to lure mates. Despite the down-to-Earth information shared previously, the whole idea is still very romantic. If you are gifted with the opportunity to watch this dancing of lights, have in mind that you are witnessing a love story.
Aaaaaawwwwwwwwwwwwww!!!

It’s patent then that even the book-warm and extremely smart witch Hermione Granger could do anything to beat them in a contest. They are amazing! They offer us an unique and thrilling experience. They are little bugs that don’t need an ancestral and mysterious book to make the “lumos” effect.

Nevertheless, this epic view could be lost forever due to the loss of natural habitat, the use of pesticides on the land (they have a larva stage) and also the light contamination, meaning the overexpression of artificial lights that affects the proper communication and thus the reproduction of these species. Is very sad to think about.

Considering that these species are associated with high conservation of natural habitat and the wellbeing of the ecosystem, the fact that they are being threatened by extinction, has called the attention of some governments. Luckily for everyone, some countries have established forest area as sanctuaries for fireflies.
Nowadays, you can enjoy the light's show at its maximum expression in The North Caves, in New Zealand; the Allegheny National Forest, in Pennsylvania, USA; Kampung Kuantan in Malaysia; La Toscana, Italy; or -closer to us- Tlaxcala in Mexico. In these places, the priority is to allow fireflies to live with the minimal interference of urbanization.
Thanks to this, we can assure that the ENCHANTED FOREST will continue existing.
